Intro

I am from a small city in India named Kaithal. I grew up with a public library inside our house made by my father for our neighborhood's general awareness. In one of the encyclopedias, I read about the Universe and fell in love with the idea of Physics. School curicullum could not feed my curiousity, I started to read on my own. I aspired to be a physicist and followed my dream by studying in famous Hans raj College of University of Delhi. However, my interests started to change as I grew tried of inefficient education system lacking research orientation or passion for the subject. From my various tech book readings, I also came to a conclusion that the most optimal way to solve physics would be to solve intelligence first, based on progress shown by Deep Learning. Therefore, I took a rational decision to change my aspired field of research to Artificial Intelligence.

My love for the abstract and unquenchable thirst for knowledge drive me to work on the problem of intelligence. Solving intelligence is beyond doubt the greatest unsolved problem. I wish to contribute in this great mission. I have developed passion in Deep Learning,Data science, Neuroscience and Machine Learning. I strive to do research in building new complex architectures of Artificial Neural Networks and use them to build an educational application that can feed every child's curiosity.

Projects

  • Exploring Activation Functions for ANNs on Image classification (April 2019 - May 2019)

    • We used Keras to implement Convolutional Neural Networks, Deep Neural Networks and Convolutional Autoencoders with RELU, GELU , Swish, SELU and RELU activation functions. We found that on MNIST and F-MNIST datasets, GELU activation function performed the best.

    • Solar Irradiance Prediction using PySpark (April 2019 - May 2019)

      • We developed a pipeline which implements a variety of machine learning algorithms for predicting solar irradiance, on Spark. The software’s design centers on execution in Google Cloud Platform along with accessing pertinent data from a Google Storage Bucket.

      • Celia Segmentation using Unets (February 2019 - March 2019)

        • We segmented Celia from 314 videos of Celia using Unets, a CNN architecture. We used python and Keras. We also preprocessed images by calculating optical flow and variance across pixels.
